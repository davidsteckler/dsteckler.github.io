Me in 10 seconds

7th grade computer science educator. Math enthusiast turned code whisperer. Seeker of difficult paths and late-night tapas. Philosopher in the classroom, adventurer at heart.

The Longer Story

Picture this: a math teacher stumbling into the world of robotics and automation. That's me, David Steckler, embarking on my 9th year of teaching computer science at Highlands. My classroom? It's where binary meets Socrates, and where we occasionally debate the ethics of teaching robots to do our homework.

Last summer, Barcelona became my classroom. Imagine dining at 10 PM, the sun still winking at you, as if to say, "Siesta well?" This Mediterranean escapade taught me the art of slowing down - a skill I'm still perfecting, especially when faced with a pile of ungraded coding assignments.

Paris wasn't just about the Eiffel Tower and croissants. It was a masterclass in the beauty of taking it slow. Sitting at a caf√©, watching the world go by, I realized life - much like coding - doesn't always need to run at breakneck speed. Sometimes, a well-placed pause (or a semicolon) makes all the difference.

Greece, oh Greece! It wasn't all mythology and moussaka. Picture me, post-emergency foot surgery, hobbling across Santorini's cobblestone streets on crutches. It was a crash course (pun intended) in perseverance and problem-solving. Who knew navigating ancient ruins could be such an adventure sport?

Recent Revelations

This summer, I had a date with destiny - and four wisdom teeth. Enter the Region-Beta Paradox: sometimes, you need to bite the bullet (or get the teeth pulled) to move forward. It was a painful reminder that procrastination has its costs, but also that we're often stronger than we think. Now, I use this experience to teach my students about tackling challenges head-on - in coding and in life.

My AI Toolkit

In the ever-evolving world of tech, AI tools are my Swiss Army knife. Here's my current lineup:

  • ChatGPT: My go-to for all-around use
  • Perplexity: My new answer engine
  • Google Search: Still king for web navigation
  • Claude: My programming first draft guru and creative writing companion
  • Kaiber.ai: My image-to-video magician
  • Topaz Video AI: My video quality enhancer
  • CapCut: My multilingual subtitle wizard

These tools aren't just gadgets; they're gateways to efficiency and creativity. In my classroom, we explore how these AI assistants can amplify our abilities, not replace them. It's not about AI doing the work for us; it's about AI helping us work smarter.
