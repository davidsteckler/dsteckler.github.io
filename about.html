About Me

I'm David Steckler. I teach computer science to 7th graders.

The Short Version

  • Math guy turned coding teacher
  • Learned to code one day ahead of my students
  • Love spontaneous challenges
  • Exploring AI in education and life

A Few Things I've Learned

1. The best way to learn is by doing. When I started teaching coding, I was barely ahead of my students. It was terrifying and exhilarating. That experience taught me more than any textbook could.

2. Technology is just a tool. What matters is how we use it. I've seen students create incredible things with basic programs. It's not about the fanciest tech, but the creativity behind it.

3. The most valuable skill is adaptability. The tech world changes fast. What's cutting-edge today might be obsolete tomorrow. Learning how to learn is more important than memorizing facts.

Current Fascinations

Right now, I'm diving deep into AI and education. I'm experimenting with creating virtual mentors and AI-powered learning tools. It's a bit like playing with fire - exciting and a little dangerous.

I'm also exploring the intersection of technology and mindfulness. How do we stay human in a digital world? It's a question I ask myself and my students often.

A Thought to Ponder

If a ship has all its parts replaced over time, is it still the same ship? Now think about your smartphone or your computer. As we constantly upgrade and change our tech, are we still the same users? Or are we evolving alongside our tools?

If any of this sparks your curiosity, feel free to explore more of my site or reach out. I'm always up for a good conversation about tech, education, or life in general.
