Here is what I wanted you to feel today:

Confusion is not proof that you cannot do something. It is usually the first room.

In CodeHS, Python did not always make sense right away. Sometimes the program looked strange. Sometimes the output surprised you. Sometimes the mistake was tiny and annoying.

But when you predicted, tested, changed one thing, fixed one mistake, and kept going, something happened: the black box started to open.

That light bulb feeling is joy. Not because the problem was easy. Because it was hard, and you stayed long enough to understand it.

Hard work does not guarantee every outcome. But becoming someone who can do hard work gives you a kind of pride no one can hand you. That pride becomes confidence.

You may not always believe you can do it at first. Choose the more useful belief: if I sit with hard things long enough, they can open.
