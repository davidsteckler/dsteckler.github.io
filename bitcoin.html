Cryptography & Bitcoin Scavenger Hunt

Cryptography Basics

What is cryptography, and what is it used for?
Cryptography is the art of creating secret codes to protect information. It ensures that only the intended people can read messages, keeping data safe in things like passwords, online chats, and banking.
How do secret codes (like shifting letters) help keep messages private?
Secret codes change letters or numbers in a message so it looks like gibberish. Only people who know the special rules (the key) can turn it back into the original message.
What is the difference between encryption and decryption?
Encryption is the process of scrambling a message to keep it secret. Decryption is turning that scrambled message back into its original form using a key.
What does a password do, and why is it an example of cryptography?
A password acts like a secret code that only you know. It verifies your identity and protects your information from others, making it a form of cryptography.
How does cryptography keep your emails or messages secure?
Cryptography turns your messages into a scrambled format. Only the person you send it to, who has the key, can unscramble and read the message, keeping it safe from eavesdroppers.

Bitcoin Fundamentals

What is Bitcoin? Describe it in one sentence.
Bitcoin is a type of digital money that uses secret codes to stay secure and operates without the need for banks.
Who created Bitcoin, and what name is used for its creator?
Bitcoin was created by someone or a group using the name Satoshi Nakamoto, but no one knows their true identity.
What problem was Bitcoin designed to solve?
Bitcoin was created to allow people to send money directly to each other without needing banks, making transactions faster and more secure.
What does it mean that Bitcoin is decentralized?
Decentralized means that no single person, bank, or company controls Bitcoin. Instead, it’s maintained by many computers around the world working together.
How does a blockchain work, and why is it important for Bitcoin?
A blockchain is like a public ledger that records every Bitcoin transaction. It ensures transparency and security because everyone can see the transactions, but no one can change them.

Digital Money & Deflation

What does it mean that Bitcoin is a deflationary asset?
Being deflationary means there’s a limited number of Bitcoin (21 million), so as more people want it, its value can increase over time.
How many total Bitcoin will ever exist?
Only 21 million Bitcoin will ever exist. This limit ensures that Bitcoin remains scarce.
What is the difference between Bitcoin and regular cash (like the dollar)?
Bitcoin is digital, has a limited supply, and isn’t controlled by any single entity, while regular cash is physical, can be printed in unlimited amounts by governments, and is centrally controlled.
Why do some people call Bitcoin “digital gold”?
Bitcoin is called “digital gold” because, like gold, it is scarce and valuable. However, Bitcoin is easier to transfer and use online.
What happens to the value of money during inflation?
During inflation, the prices of things go up, which means the money you have can buy less than before.

Real-World Applications

How does Bitcoin help people who don’t have access to a bank?
Bitcoin allows anyone with internet access to send and receive money without needing a traditional bank, effectively acting as a portable bank.
What are some countries or companies using Bitcoin today?
Countries like El Salvador use Bitcoin as official money, and companies like Tesla and MicroStrategy hold Bitcoin as an investment.
How can Bitcoin be used to send money across the world?
Bitcoin can be sent instantly to anyone, anywhere in the world, without waiting for banks to process the transaction, making it faster and often cheaper.
Why is Bitcoin considered secure even though it’s online?
Bitcoin uses strong cryptography and a decentralized network to protect transactions, making it very secure and hard to hack.
Name one limitation or challenge of using Bitcoin.
One challenge of using Bitcoin is that its price can be very volatile, meaning it can go up and down a lot in a short time.

MicroStrategy & Digital Manhattan

What is MicroStrategy, and who leads the company?
MicroStrategy is a technology company that invests in Bitcoin, and it is led by Michael Saylor, who is a big believer in Bitcoin’s future.
Why did MicroStrategy decide to invest in Bitcoin?
MicroStrategy invested in Bitcoin because they believe it’s a better way to protect their money compared to holding cash, which loses value over time.
How much Bitcoin has MicroStrategy acquired so far?
MicroStrategy has bought hundreds of thousands of Bitcoin, making it one of the largest companies holding Bitcoin.
What is MicroStrategy’s main argument for holding Bitcoin instead of cash or bonds?
Michael Saylor argues that cash loses value due to inflation, and bonds aren’t performing well, whereas Bitcoin has the potential to grow in value.
What does Michael Saylor mean when he says Bitcoin is “digital capital”?
Saylor refers to Bitcoin as “digital capital” because it’s a valuable asset that exists online, easy to transfer, and cannot be destroyed.
What is the idea of “Digital Manhattan” that Michael Saylor talks about?
“Digital Manhattan” is the concept that owning Bitcoin is like owning prime digital real estate, similar to having valuable property in a prestigious location like Manhattan.
How is Bitcoin like owning a piece of real estate in a city like Manhattan?
Just as land in Manhattan is limited and highly sought after, Bitcoin is scarce and valuable, making it a desirable asset to own.
Why does Saylor believe Bitcoin has advantages over traditional real estate (like buildings)?
Bitcoin doesn’t have issues like taxes, maintenance, or weather damage. It’s also easy to transfer and can be used globally without physical limitations.
What are some benefits of Bitcoin that make it “digital, indestructible, and global”?
Bitcoin is digital, meaning it exists online; indestructible, as it can’t be physically damaged or erased; and global, allowing it to be used anywhere in the world without intermediaries.
Why does Saylor call Bitcoin the next wave of technology, following trends like the internet and AI?
Saylor believes Bitcoin will revolutionize money and capital just as the internet changed communication and AI is transforming technology, making it a pivotal advancement for the future.
