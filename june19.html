The Magic of Spontaneity: A Juneteenth to Remember

Yesterday was a day of unexpected joy. With Juneteenth off, I had a simple plan: walk our dog Pixie, hit the gym, and relax. But the gym was packed, every chair taken. It was clear that today needed a different approach.

Leigh’s gym friend told us about her partner’s boat rental club. Seizing the moment, we met them at Stamford Harbor near PRIME. After a quick walk and feeding Pixie (wishing she could join us), we set off on a boat ride from Stamford to a small island off Greenwich.

Being on the water was liberating. The wind in my face, the boat slicing through the waves, the horizon stretching out endlessly—it was pure bliss. We saw Tod’s Point Beach, the lively Greenwich Avenue, and the Delamar Hotel where my sister got married. Familiar sights from a fresh perspective.

Docking was a challenge. No mooring spots were available. After some humorous attempts to anchor, we finally secured a spot at Great Captains Island. The island was buzzing with activity, boats all around, people running, and laughing. Leigh swam to another boat and asked if she could jump off their fourth floor. They said yes! I was nervous, imagining all the ways it could go wrong. While waiting, I swam in the ocean for about 20 minutes. It was exhausting—more tiring than I expected.

Leigh made the jump, and I captured it on camera. A man with a cigar, sunbathing on the deck, clapped. Everyone cheered. The whole scene was surreal. It struck me how boats offer a unique freedom, a world apart from our daily routines.

Our conversations on the boat were enriching. We shared books that had moved us. I talked about Derek Sivers’ "How to Live," a profound journey through various philosophies of living. Darren shared "Killing Lincoln" by Bill O'Reilly, a gripping account of the assassination of President Lincoln. Karen introduced us to "The Mountain Is You" by Brianna Wiest, which dives into self-sabotage and personal growth. Leigh, not much of a reader, still enjoyed the conversation.

Seeing a boat with dogs made me think of Pixie and the fun she’d have. Though she needs more practice in the car, the idea of her on a boat is exciting.

This day was a reminder of the power of spontaneity. Reflecting on Juneteenth, I realize the importance of creating memories that stick. Writing this at 10:38 PM, with my wife and dog asleep beside me, I’m grateful. The day ended simply, with a meal from CVS and an episode of Stranger Things. Yet, it was perfect.

There’s no such thing as a bad day on the water. Yesterday was a testament to living fully and embracing the unexpected.
